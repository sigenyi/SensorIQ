# JAZZ AI IMAGE QUALITY SUCCESS CRITERIA
# Purpose: Define what a "good" radiograph looks like in objective, structure-based terms.
# This gives the AI a self-validation framework so success is not purely subjective.
# Source: Dental radiology standards + lamina dura / PDL space visibility research.

---

## SECTION 1: THE FIVE DIAGNOSTIC STRUCTURES

A radiograph is considered diagnostically successful when these structures are visible
and readable according to the criteria below. Match the criteria to the diagnostic goal.

---

### STRUCTURE 1: Lamina Dura (LD)
- APPEARANCE: A continuous, thin, radiopaque (white) line lining the tooth socket.
- DIAGNOSTIC VALUE: Confirms healthy periodontal attachment. Loss indicates pathology.
- QUALITY CRITERION: LD should appear as a distinct, unbroken bright line.
  It should be visually separable from the surrounding trabecular bone.
- PHYSICS NOTE: LD is thin cortical bone (~0.1-0.5mm). It requires adequate kVp
  penetration AND sufficient contrast to distinguish from adjacent structures.
  High kVp (>75) risks washing out LD visibility — it blends into surrounding gray.
- SOFTWARE OPTIMIZATION: CLAHE (local contrast) is the primary tool for LD visibility.
  Clip Limit 2.0-2.5 helps isolate the dense cortical line from cancellous bone.
- FAILURE MODE: LD disappears or merges with bone → image is diagnostically inadequate.

---

### STRUCTURE 2: Periodontal Ligament (PDL) Space
- APPEARANCE: A thin, even radiolucent (dark) line between the root and lamina dura.
  Clinical width: 0.15-0.38mm in health.
- DIAGNOSTIC VALUE: PDL widening indicates inflammation, trauma, or occlusal pathology.
  PDL space is one of the most diagnostically sensitive targets in periapical imaging.
- QUALITY CRITERION: The PDL space should be visible as a hairline dark line,
  consistently visible along at least 2/3 of the root length.
- PHYSICS NOTE: PDL space is extremely thin. If OFD is high (geometric blur) or
  sharpening is insufficient, the PDL space will not be resolved.
- SOFTWARE OPTIMIZATION:
  - Sharpening Sigma: 3.0-5.0 (enough radius to detect the thin structure)
  - Sharpening Weight: 0.75-1.0 (enough emphasis to make it distinct)
  - Gamma: 0.55-0.65 (lifts shadows to reveal the dark PDL line)
  - S-Curve: 0.70-0.85 (Soft Clip re-introduces tail data where PDL lives)
- FAILURE MODE: PDL line not visible or continuous → image insufficient for perio diagnosis.

---

### STRUCTURE 3: Trabecular Bone Pattern
- APPEARANCE: A lattice-like network of mixed radiopaque (trabeculae) and radiolucent
  (marrow spaces) regions throughout the cancellous bone.
- DIAGNOSTIC VALUE: Trabecular texture change indicates systemic disease, healing,
  or pathology. It is the "background" against which caries and lesions are detected.
- QUALITY CRITERION: The trabecular pattern should show differentiated density —
  visible variation between bright trabeculae and dark marrow spaces.
  A "flat gray soup" appearance means local contrast is insufficient.
- SOFTWARE OPTIMIZATION:
  - CLAHE is the primary tool. Acts as a "microscope" for trabecular detail.
  - Clip Limit: 2.0-2.50 for perio/pathology work.
  - Recipe C (Perio): 70+ kVp | CLAHE-focused | Sharpen Weight 1.0.
- FAILURE MODE: Trabecular detail is uniform gray → CLAHE insufficient or kVp too high.

---

### STRUCTURE 4: Alveolar Crest
- APPEARANCE: The coronal edge of the alveolar bone between adjacent teeth.
- DIAGNOSTIC CRITERION: Healthy alveolar crest is 1.5-2.0mm below the
  Cemento-Enamel Junction (CEJ). Bone loss is measurable above this threshold.
- QUALITY CRITERION: The crest should appear as a defined radiopaque edge,
  not a blurred or indistinct boundary.
- FAILURE MODE: Crest boundary blurred → OFD too high or sharpening insufficient.

---

### STRUCTURE 5: Caries (Decay Detection)
- APPEARANCE: Radiolucent (dark) area within enamel or dentin.
  Interproximal caries appears as a triangular dark region at the contact point.
- QUALITY CRITERION: For caries detection, the image must show:
  - High contrast between enamel (very bright) and decay (dark).
  - Sharp interproximal interfaces.
- SOFTWARE OPTIMIZATION:
  - Recipe A (Caries): 60-65 kVp | CLAHE 2.5 | Sharpen Weight 2.0 | Gamma 0.8.
  - Low kVp is preferred because it maximizes enamel contrast.
- FAILURE MODE: Enamel and decay have similar gray values → kVp too high.

## SECTION 2: DIAGNOSTIC GOAL → SUCCESS CRITERIA MATRIX

| Diagnostic Goal      | Must-See Structures       | Key Settings to Verify             |
|----------------------|---------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Caries detection     | Enamel contrast, Dentin   | Low kVp (60-65), High CLAHE        |
| Perio bone levels    | Alveolar crest, LD, Trab. | High kVp (70+), CLAHE, Gamma ~0.65 |
| Endo (root canal)    | Apex, Canal, PDL space    | Gamma 0.5, High Sigma sharpening   |
| Fracture detection   | PDL space, Crown cracks   | S-Curve 0.85-0.90, Gamma 0.40-0.50 |
| Post-op / healing    | Trabecular pattern        | Balanced: CLAHE 2.0, Gamma 0.65    |

---

## SECTION 3: MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE IMAGE STANDARD

An image is NOT diagnostically acceptable if ANY of the following are true:
  - Large areas of pure white with no internal structure (sensor saturation)
  - Large areas of pure black with no visible anatomy (underexposure)
  - Lamina dura is completely invisible across all teeth in the image
  - PDL space cannot be resolved on any root surface
  - Trabecular bone appears uniformly gray (no differentiation)
  - Geometric blur is so severe that root apices are not distinguishable

If an image fails any of the above, log the session as "reshoot required" and
escalate to hardware settings adjustment before returning to software corrections.

---

## SECTION 4: HOW TO USE LOGGED SUCCESS DATA

When a calibration is logged as successful in Google Sheets, the accompanying
settings string represents a VALIDATED parameter set for that software + machine
combination at specific hardware settings (kVp / mA / exposure).

VALID BASELINE CONDITIONS:
  - The logged settings are valid for similar hardware (±5 kVp, ±1 mA, ±0.02s).
  - If hardware changes significantly, treat as a new baseline.
  - A successful log does NOT mean the settings are optimal for a different
    diagnostic goal (e.g., caries settings are not optimal for endo).

LOG QUALITY SIGNAL:
  - "Client happy" = subjective acceptance. Moderate confidence.
  - "Lamina dura visible, PDL confirmed" = objective acceptance. High confidence.
  - Prefer logging with structure-specific confirmations for higher-quality training data.
