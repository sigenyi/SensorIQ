--- SECTION 1: RADIOGRAPHIC EXPOSURE PHYSICS (THE ACQUISITION LAYER) ---

# EXPOSURE_FACTOR_QUANTITY: mAs (Milliampere-seconds)
- DEFINITION: Product of tube current (mA) and exposure time (s).
- FUNCTION: Controls the total volume (quantity) of radiation.
- UNDER-EXPOSURE (Too Low): Image is too light; high "Quantum Mottle" (graininess/noise).
- OVER-EXPOSURE (Too High): Image is too dark (burnout).
- AI_REFORMATTING_LOGIC: If mAs is low, AI must use aggressive Denoising but conservative CLAHE to avoid amplifying artifacts.

# EXPOSURE_FACTOR_QUALITY: kV (Kilovoltage)
- DEFINITION: Voltage across the tube; determines photon energy (penetration).
- PENETRATION: Higher kV penetrates dense tissue better but reduces soft tissue contrast.
- LOW_KV EFFECT: High contrast; distinct black/white levels.
- HIGH_KV EFFECT: Low contrast; image appears "Uniformly Gray."
- OVER-PENETRATION: Excessively high kV passes straight through to detector, "washing out" all anatomical detail.

# GEOMETRIC_INTEGRITY: DISTANCE_FACTORS
- FFD (Focal-Film Distance): Affects magnification and intensity. 
  - RULE: A 20cm increase in FFD requires doubling the mAs (Inverse Square Law approximation).
- OFD (Object-Film Distance): The gap between patient and sensor.
  - RULE: Minimize OFD to prevent geometric unsharpness/blurring (The "Hand-Shadow" principle).
  - AI_REFORMATTING_LOGIC: High OFD requires higher "Sigma" sharpening to compensate for penumbra.

--- SECTION 2: ANATOMICAL LANDMARKS & DENSITY PROFILES ---

# RADIOPACITY_LEVELS (Visual Logic)
- RADIOPAQUE (White/Light): Dense structures (Cortical bone, Enamel, Septa, Metal).
- RADIOLUCENT (Dark/Black): Less dense (Cancellous bone marrow, Pulp, PDL space, Sinuses, Caries).

# MANDIBULAR_KEY_POINTS
- Genial Tubercles: Ring-shaped radiopacity below incisor apices.
- Lingual Foramen: Radiolucent dot inside the genial tubercle ring.
- Mental Foramen: Ovoid radiolucency near premolar apices (Note: Do not confuse with pathology).
- Mandibular Canal: Radiolucent band with thin radiopaque cortical walls.

# MAXILLARY_KEY_POINTS
- Incisive Foramen: Ovoid radiolucency between central incisors.
- Maxillary Sinus: Large radiolucent area; floor appears as dense cortical bone.
- Inverted Y: Intersection of nasal fossa and maxillary sinus (Critical landmark).
- Zygomatic Process: U or J-shaped radiopacity superior to first molar.

# TOOTH_SUPPORT_STRUCTURES
- Lamina Dura: Dense radiopaque line of the tooth socket.
- PDL Space: Thin radiolucent line between root and lamina dura (Diagnostic target for AI).
- Alveolar Crest: Coronal bone (Healthy = 1.5–2.0mm below CEJ).

--- SECTION 3: DIGITAL REFORMATTING ALGORITHMS & PIPELINE ---

# REFORMATTING_ORDER_OF_OPERATIONS (Critical for AI)
1. ADAPTIVE_NORMALIZATION: Establishes global structure (Non-linear).
2. CLAHE: Enhances local detail/contrast (Non-linear, tile-based).
3. GAMMA_CORRECTION: Controls mid-tone perception without clipping.
4. SHARPENING: Edge emphasis (Unsharp masking).
5. CONTRAST/BRIGHTNESS: Final linear tuning.

# ALGORITHM_LOGIC_&_RISKS
- Adaptive Normalization: 
  - Strengths: Compensates for mAs/kV variations. 
  - Risks: Can flatten images if dynamic range is low.
- CLAHE (Local Detail): 
  - Strengths: Acts as a "microscope" for trabecular bone.
  - Risks: High "Clip Limit" amplifies noise (Quantum Mottle).
- Sharpening (Unsharp Mask):
  - Logic: High Weight = High Edge Emphasis.
  - Risks: "Halo Artifacts" around metal or dense enamel; can create "False Fractures."
- Gamma:
  - Logic: < 1.0 (Brightens mid-tones); > 1.0 (Darkens mid-tones).
  - Use: Corrects for over-penetration (High kV) or under-exposure (Low mAs).

--- SECTION 4: AI DIAGNOSTIC DECISION MATRIX ---

> IF Image_Grainy (Low mAs): 
    - PRIORITY: Noise Reduction > Normalization. 
    - AVOID: High Sharpening (amplifies noise).

> IF Image_Uniform_Gray (High kV): 
    - PRIORITY: Increase Contrast + Adjust Gamma (>1.0).
    - AVOID: Over-Normalization (may wash out gray values further).

> IF Image_Blurry (High OFD):
    - PRIORITY: Edge-detection sharpening with low Sigma.
    - GOAL: Compensate for geometric penumbra.

> IF Bone_Pathology_Suspected:
    - PRIORITY: CLAHE (High Clip Limit) to isolate trabecular spicules.
